POC VOICES

In order to build empathy for one another, individuals of all backgrounds must share their story. With empathy and understanding for other people, we can sort through social issues with compromises benefitting ALL people.

According to a 2020 New York Times article “Just How White is the Book Industry?” only around 10% of books are written by people of color.

"It’s been more than 50 years since literacy experts first stressed the need for more diverse books in the classroom, and yet reading lists look surprisingly the same as they did in 1970" - Kim Parker, co-founder of #Disrupttexts which suggests ways in which educators can incorporate diverse books into their curriculum — and how parents can be supportive allies in the process.

"People teach what they’re comfortable with, so the choices become this narrow realm of what you liked and what you’re familiar with” - Senior Lecturer Pamela Mason, director of the Ed School’s Language and Literacy Program.

“When there are enough books available that can act as both mirrors and windows for all our children, they will see that we can celebrate both our difference and our similarities, because together they are what makes us all human.” - Rudine Sims Bishop, in her 1990 publication Perspectives: Choosing and Using Books for the Classroom.
